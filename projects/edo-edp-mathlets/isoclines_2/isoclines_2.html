Isoclines

An isocline of a differential equation of the form

y′ = f(x, y)

is the set of points (x, y) in the plane where the slope satisfies

f(x, y) = m,

for some fixed value m. Along an isocline, all solution curves have the same slope (yellow curve on the left).

Isoclines help visualize and identify regions of growth, decay, or critical points.

Slope field

The slope field of

y′ = f(x, y)

is built by drawing, at each point (x, y), a short segment whose inclination equals the slope f(x, y).

This field provides a visual representation of the local behavior of solutions without solving the equation explicitly.

The numerical integral curves you add by clicking the plot follow the direction indicated by these segments.

How to use

Adjust a and m. The blue contour is the isocline where f(x,y)=m. Click the plot to add a numerical solution curve. Use the ✖ buttons to remove curves.
